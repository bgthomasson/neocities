Petifer and Potawatomi

Around 1700 the Beaver Wars had ended and the Potawatomi, by then incorporating various tribal refugees and French traders, spread around the southern end of Lake Michigan. Population from 4,000 to 8,000.

1833- Treaty of Chicago starts taking that land from them. A number of them go to NW Missouri, the Platte “territory”, until 1837 when they go on to SW Iowa. However they disliked that area and were allowed to go to eastern Kansas along the Osage River, at first simply the ones from Indiana were sent there, but some from Michigan went also. There they all settled into agriculture. Others were up near Topeka with the Kickapoo, who would mostly become the Prairie band. Over the next two decades around 6000 are documented going west, with up to a third heading back east at some point.
